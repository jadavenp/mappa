## Key finding: block layout (read the full sheet, not just the crops)

The supplied crops (`a1916_block28/29/40.jpg`) each show **two lot rows
split by a mid-block service alley**, with the block number printed in that
alley gap — both rows belong to the *same* block, not two separate blocks.
This was only resolved by reading the full `anchorage_1916_s1.jpg` sheet
index (all six blocks + avenue labels at once): block 28 and 29 each have a
3rd-Ave-fronting row (lots 1–12) and a 4th-Ave-fronting row (lots ~41–56);
block 40 has a 4th-Ave-fronting row (lots 1–16, including the Empress
Theatre/Bank/Alaska Building/Fowler Building concrete complex) and a
5th-Ave-fronting row (lots ~41–52). This resolves the acceptance
criterion's "Empress Theatre … block 40" — the theatre is in block 40's own
4th-Ave row, not a neighboring block as an initial reading of the crop in
isolation suggested. Full reasoning and the ASCII layout diagram are in
`anchorage-downtown.grid.md`.

Both blocks' full frontages were traced (not just the row a narrowly-cropped
image might suggest is "the" block), since GA1's charge is "every
distinguishable building in blocks 28/29/40," and both rows are genuinely
that block.

## Grid constants (7a)

`BLOCK_W` = 260 ft = 79.248 m (H↔G / I↔H), `BLOCK_D` = 300 ft = 91.44 m
(avenue-to-avenue), `AVE_W` = 80 ft = 24.384 m, `ST_W` = 60 ft = 18.288 m.
Cross-checked against the Scale-of-Feet bar visible on the `a1916_block40`
crop (measured directly: three ~300px segments = 50ft each → ~6.0 px/ft at
that crop's resolution, consistent with the plan's round-number
approximation). Block 28's SW corner is local-meter origin `(0,0)`; block 29
sits west across an 18.288m H-Street gap; block 40 sits south across a
24.384m 4th-Ave gap. Full derivation in the grid doc.

## Legibility / judgment calls (for reviewer)

1. **Generic descriptors vs proper names.** Single-word business-type labels
   legible on the sheet but not proper names (e.g. "Shoes", "Rest.",
   "Tailor", "D.G.", "Clo.", "Gro.", "Stge.", "Blsm.") were treated as `use`
   tags, `names: []` — not invented into a proper name. Multi-word or
   clearly-branded labels (Frisco Café, California Pool Hall, Matanuska
   Hardware Co., etc.) went into `names[]`.
2. **Multi-tenant concrete blocks folded to one Feature each.** The
   Bank/Moving-Pictures/Offices concrete mass (lots 11–12, under the
   "EMPRESS THEATRE BLD'G" marquee label) was modeled as **one** Feature,
   "Empress Theatre Building," rather than three; similarly "Alaska
   Building" (lots 14–16) and a separate small "Fowler Building" unit were
   kept apart since the sheet labels them as visually distinct footprints.
   Block 29's "Empress Building" (3-story, Off/Club Rm/D.G./Drugs) was
   likewise folded to one Feature.
3. **Footprint positions are lot-index-proportional, not pixel-traced.**
   Given the volume (86 buildings), each footprint's x-position/width is
   derived from its lot index and a uniform per-row lot width
   (`BLOCK_W / lot_count`), and depths are size-class estimates (4m sheds,
   8–10m shopfronts, 14–18m anchors) rather than individually
   pixel-measured — acceptable at the plan's stated precision ("out of
   scope: georeferenced accuracy beyond block-level layout"), but a
   reviewer doing a tight pixel-for-pixel recount against the crops will
   find positions approximate, not exact.
4. **Rear/interior structures** (sheds, secondary dwellings, unlabeled small
   squares) are placed at a fixed offset (~22–28m) behind their block's
   frontage line rather than at their true interior depth, purely to
   guarantee no spatial overlap with the frontage row — this is a
   simplification, not a traced position.
5. **"EMPRESS" text bleed in the `a1916_block28.jpg` crop** (bottom-right,
   near the Garage) was judged to be bleed-through from block 29's "Empress
   Bldg" label (which sits at that block's adjoining NE corner) rather than
   a street name — block 28/29's shared south frontage is "4th Ave" per the
   unambiguous label on the `a1916_block29.jpg` crop. This doesn't affect
   any Feature tag (street names aren't asserted on Features), only the
   grid doc's prose.
